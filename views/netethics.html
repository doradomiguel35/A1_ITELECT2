What is Internet Ethics

  • androidClick Here

    Is a set of rules or moral principles that governs an individual on how he/she must behave when using the computer or the internet, internet ethics varies from knowing what are privacy violations, copyright issues and many more. Internet Ethics can be also be related to actual morality on how someone is behaving in the actual world and can be inheritted to how he/she's behaving in the digital world.

    As we are facing in a world where technology evolves everyday and people are now dependent on the internet, Internet Ethics can be violated numerous times, and a lot of it are severe and in most cases people end up as violators of the law of Internet Ethics.





10 Commandment of Internet Ethics

    Thou shalt not use a computer to harm other people.

    Thou shalt not interfere with other's computer work.

    Thou shalt not snoop around in other 's computer files.

    Thou shalt not use a computer to steal.

    Thou shalt not use a computer to bear false witness.

    Thou shalt not copy or use proprietary software for which one have not paid.

    Thou shalt not use other's computer resources without authorization or proper compensation.

    Thou shalt not appropriate other's intellectual output.

    Thou shalt think about the social consequences of the program written or of the system designed by one.

    Thou shalt always use a computer in ways that insure consideration and respect for one's fellow humans.
